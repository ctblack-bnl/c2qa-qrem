**Alternative explanations:** Substrate-dependent phase stabilization (α vs β-Ta fraction) is the most plausible alternative driver of the observed Tc variation, as substrate material and surface chemistry are well-established determinants of Ta crystal phase in thin films. Differences in deposition system base pressure, argon pressure, and film thickness — none of which are matched across samples — represent additional uncontrolled variables that could fully account for the 0.2 K Tc spread.

**QREM implications:** A QREM materials-to-device mapping function relating deposition temperature to Tc for Ta is not supportable from current data. Even the sign of the relationship cannot be confirmed independently of substrate effects. If a real effect exists, it is likely mediated through phase fraction (α vs β-Ta) rather than being a direct temperature-Tc mapping, which would imply the relevant QREM parameter is phase identity or phase fraction rather than deposition temperature per se. Until controlled data are available, any QREM layer incorporating deposition temperature as a Tc predictor for Ta should treat this as an unvalidated prior with high uncertainty, and should not propagate a specific functional form (e.g., linear or monotonic) to downstream device performance estimates.

With only 3 samples and severe confounding, a focused re-analysis of Ta alone is not yet warranted — the limiting factor is data quality and experimental control, not analysis approach. Re-analysis should be triggered only after ingestion of additional Ta samples that include XRD phase identification and share at least one controlled substrate-system pair.
